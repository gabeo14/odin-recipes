Old Fashioned

Cocktail Description

In the latter half of the 19th century during the cocktail golden era some were left longing for the days before complexity and showmanship were the defining characteristics of the cocktail bars of the day. The most popular theory for now is that these nostalgic patrons would ask for a drink "the old fashioned way" and that over time that just became "Old fasioned."

The primary ingredients of the drink being simply whiskey and sugar does lend some creedence to this theory. You'll see many variations on the recipe, with some using sugar cubes, others using simple syrup, and you may even see some where you muddle an orange slice.

Ingredients

  • 2oz Bourbon or Rye
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 4 dashes Angosturra Bitters
  • 1 Maraschino cherry (pref. Luxardo, optional garnish)
  • 1 peel of orange(optional garnish)
  • 1 peel of lemon(optional garnish)

Instructions

  1. Place sugar cube in bottom of double rocks glass
  2. add 4 dashes of Angostura bitters directly to sugar cube
  3. Muddle sugar cube and bitters until texture is a grainy syrup
  4. Add 2oz of chosen whiskey to glass
  5. Place large rock of ice in glass and stir for 45-60 seconds
  6. Add cherry to glass
  7. Express oils from orange and lemon peels and add peels to glass or discard
  8. Enjoy!
